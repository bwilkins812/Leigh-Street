A History of the Crown and Basket

The origins of the Crown and Basket can be traced back to an earlier establishment on the premises known as The Hollerith Inn. The Hollerith was a terminus for stagecoaches coming into Chicago up through the Civil War. With the decline of coach travel and the increase of rail travel, the Hollerith was struggling financially and as fate would have it, the inn was largely destroyed in the Great Chicago Fire of 1871. All that remained were three rooms on the ground floor and a part of the tavern. You can still see remnants of the original building along the south wall. The part of the building that survived was repaired and became the Neumann Restaurant in 1873. It was a well-known establishment and was frequently visited by railroad magnates and dignitaries. President Grant, during a visit in 1874, famously quipped that he was "finished with State dinners at the White House. I'm going to Neumann's." Mr. Neumann sold his restaurant to the current owners in 1921 who renamed it the Crown and Basket after two components of the outfield wall of nearby Wrigley Field.
